INTRODUCTION

The analysis reveals significant trends in urban land dynamics, highlighting the importance of geometric assessments in urban planning. Hyderabad's urban expansion shows strongest growth in the northwest (0→150 units) and north (peaking at 200 units), driven by IT hubs like Hi-Tech City, while east and west show moderate growth. The south and southeast remain stagnant, likely due to geographic constraints or dense urban cores. This pattern highlights a clear northward expansion bias, requiring focused infrastructure planning in high-growth areas while maintaining southern stability.
